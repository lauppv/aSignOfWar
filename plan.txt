NOTE: NOT every mechanic is written here. Many decisions were made along the way.

A clone of Tribal Wars set in the modern era. The source code and the website are in EN for consistency; this document is in EN as well.

Each player starts with a single city and:
1) 10 Heavy
2) 10 Light infantry
3) 1000 of each resource
4) Headquarters Level 1
5) Power plant Level 1
6) Bank Level 1
7) Weapons factory Level 1
8) Military base Level 0 (requires Headquarters Level 5)
9) Housing Level 1
10) Warehouse Level 1
11) Harbor Level 0 (requires Headquarters Level 15)
12) Air defense Level 0 (requires Headquarters Level 5)


Units and resources are per city, not per account.
There is no Smithy. Units are unlocked as Headquarters and Military base grow in Level (see Units + unlocking).


Resource types:
1) Money (produced by Bank) - Wood in Tribal Wars
2) Energy (produced by Power plant - Clay in Tribal Wars)
3) Ammo (produced by Weapons factory - Iron in Tribal Wars)

Buildings (with a mapping onto Tribal Wars):
1) Headquarters (Main building). Max Level 30
2) Bank (Timber camp). Max Level 30
3) Power plant (Clay pit). Max Level 30
4) Weapons factory (Iron mine). Max Level 30
5) Military base (barracks + stable). Max Level 25
6) Housing (Farm (population)). Max Level 30
7) Warehouse (Warehouse (stores resources)). Max Level 30
8) Harbor (market (to send resources to other cities)). Max Level 25
9) Air defense (Wall). Special building that does not appear in the city image, but has its own card/component (probably below the panel with the city's unit counts). Max Level 20


Units (with a mapping onto Tribal Wars) + unlocking (Headquarters Level):
1) Attack:
    Light infantry (axe fighter) - no Headquarters requirement
    Raider (light cavalry) - Headquarters Level 10
    Special forces (mounted archer) - Headquarters Level 15 + Military base Level 10
2) Defense:
    Heavy infantry (spearman) - no Headquarters requirement
    Defender infantry (swordsman) - Headquarters Level 5
    Sniper (archer) - Headquarters Level 10 + Military base Level 10
3) Mix:
    Tank (heavy cavalry) - Headquarters Level 20 + Military base Level 15
4) Siege:
    Missile launcher (ram) - Headquarters Level 20 + Military base Level 15
    Drone (catapult) - Headquarters Level 20 + Military base Level 20

Unit types:
    1) Infantry: Light infantry, Defender infantry, Heavy infantry
    2) Range: Sniper, Special forces
    3) Mechanized: Raider, Tank
    4) Siege: Missile launcher, Drone
    5) Conquer: Governor


All units are recruited from the Military base.

We will also have a map with coordinates. To send commands (attack, support, resources) to a city, there will be a Command field where you select the command type (military (attack, support) or resources) and the target player's name. If the player exists, show their cities and you select the target city. Depending on the command type, the component shows different information:
1) attack/support:
    city name
    owner name
    where the command comes from (your city)
    travel time (without a map we assume every command takes 60 seconds)
    command arrival time (updated in real time, every second, like in Tribal Wars, for attack/support timing)
    which troops are about to be sent
2) resources:
    Energy amount
    Money amount
    Ammo amount
    travel time (without a map, 60 seconds)
    arrival time (same as for attack/support)
If the ID is not valid, the component shows a simple message "the city with ID X does not exist"

Every city has something called Loyalty (adhesion in Tribal Wars). Every city starts with Loyalty = 100%. When it drops below 0%, the city is conquered. To lower Loyalty you need one or more Governors. A Governor can lower Loyalty by 20-35%, and this only happens if the attacked city has no defending unit at all. A Governor has no offensive or defensive values, but occupies 100 population.
To recruit a Governor, Headquarters must be at Level 30 (max). It is recruited from Headquarters. In Headquarters we have the count of current Governors in the city. Since we don't have coins like in Tribal Wars, each Governor costs exponentially more. There will be 3 progress bars that must be filled with the 3 resources (like the Wonders of the World in Grepolis or the legendary building levels in Forge of Empires).

The Governor counter is PER ACCOUNT (not per city). That is, the Nth Governor recruited on the account costs the same regardless of which city you recruit it from — including Governors lost in failed attacks, which stay counted (the counter does not reset). The 3 recipients (Money/Energy/Ammo) accept partial deposits from any of your own cities: you can contribute gradually until all 3 bars are full, at which point the Governor is recruited in the city where you completed the last bar.

The cost of each resource for the Nth Governor (Money = Energy = Ammo):

  Governor 1:    10,000
  Governor 2:    15,000
  Governor 3:    22,500
  Governor 4:    35,000
  Governor 5:    55,000
  Governor 6:   100,000
  Governor 7:   190,000
  Governor 8:   370,000
  Governor 9:   730,000
  Governor 10: 1,460,000
  ...

Formula: for N ≤ 5, cost(N) = round(10_000 * 1.5^(N-1)); for N ≥ 6, cost(N) = cost(5) * 2^(N-5). The first 5 grow by ~×1.5 (affordable with 1-2 well-developed cities), then from the 6th the curve doubles (expansion pressure — you need more cities producing simultaneously).

================================================================================
GHOST CITIES (cities with no owner)
================================================================================

Ghost cities are neutral, ownerless cities, generated automatically around each
newly founded city. For every new player created in a world, 3 Ghost cities are
generated. These cities can be conquered by other players.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Initial template (at generation)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Buildings:
  Headquarters     Level 3
  Bank             Level 3
  Power plant      Level 3
  Weapons factory  Level 3
  Housing          Level 3
  Warehouse        Level 3
  Military base    Level 0
  Harbor           Level 0
  Air defense      Level 0

Resources: full (equal to the capacity of Warehouse Level 3 = 1511 of each).
Loyalty: 100%.
Units: none (the ghost city has no initial army).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Resource production
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Resources grow normally, following the same formulas as any other city (based on
the Bank/Power plant/Weapons factory Levels). They are capped at the current
Warehouse capacity. When the city is conquered, the player takes over the
resources as of that moment.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Building auto-upgrade (passive growth)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
At a regular interval, the ghost city picks a RANDOM building and raises its
Level by 1 (it does NOT enqueue build commands, it applies Level+1 directly):

  - tick interval: every ~6h (to be calibrated based on game speed)
  - eligible buildings: ALL 9 buildings of the city — including Military
    base and Air defense. The only restriction is that the ghost city cannot
    recruit units (see the "Army" section).
      Headquarters, Bank, Power plant, Weapons factory, Housing, Warehouse,
      Military base, Harbor, Air defense
  - selection: uniform random over the buildings eligible in the current tick
  - respects all HQ requirements (e.g. Harbor requires HQ Level 15, Military base
    and Air defense require HQ Level 5). A building blocked by HQ is excluded from
    the selection pool for the current tick.
  - FREE: the auto-upgrade does NOT consume the ghost city's resources. Reason:
    players will constantly farm these cities, so they will rarely have enough
    resources; if we charged real costs, the passive growth would effectively
    stall and the goal (ever-fatter targets) would not be achieved.
  - stops when all buildings are at maxLevel

Effect: even if nobody conquers it, the ghost city keeps growing in score and in
defense (Air defense). The longer it is left alone, the fatter a target it
becomes (more points on conquest) and the harder it is to crack (big Air defense
bonus).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Army (forbidden)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The ghost city CANNOT recruit units. The Military base grows in score/Level, but
produces no troops. As a consequence:
  - the ghost city never sends commands (attack, support, resources, spy)
  - having no defending units (unless supported by a player), any Governor sent
    against the ghost reduces its Loyalty by 20-35% (the standard conquest rule)
  - after conquest, the buildings stay at their current Levels, but the
    new-owner player starts recruiting normally from the Military base

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To calibrate
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - the auto-upgrade interval (6h is just a starting point; it depends on the
    average duration of a world and on game speed)
  - whether auto-upgrade accounts for game speed (probably YES: tick = 6h / gameSpeed)

To spy, you send hackers. This is a command different from attack, called spy.
The mechanic is hacker vs hacker. A hacker ignores the defending units in the target city. The battle is hacker vs hacker.
If the target city has 100 hackers, you need at least 101 hackers to be able to spy on the city. Of the 101 attacking hackers, 1 will survive. So you need at least one surviving hacker to spy successfully. The defending hackers will NOT die.

================================================================================
MAP AND COORDINATES
================================================================================

The world is a 100x100 grid (configurable). Each city occupies exactly one cell,
identified by an (x, y) pair. The coordinates are the "real" primary key of a
city in the game: names can repeat across players, but (x, y) is unique.
That is why the development scripts (see server/scripts/dev-cheats.ts) take "x,y"
as an argument, not a name — the name "ORAS2" can exist for two different players.

The client-side map (MapPage) draws the grid as a repeating-linear-gradient to
avoid tens of thousands of DOM nodes, and renders each city as a sprite chosen
based on its score (0-299, 300-999, 1000-2999, 3000-8999, 9000+).
The action panel that appears on click (CityActionPanel) is the same regardless
of whether the city is yours, the alliance's (TODO), another player's, or a ghost —
only the buttons and labels change depending on the relationship.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Distance and travel times
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The distance between two cities is measured by Euclidean distance, in "fields":

    distance = sqrt((x2 - x1)^2 + (y2 - y1)^2)

For military commands (ATTACK, SUPPORT, SPY), travel time is computed exactly as
in Tribal Wars: take the slowest unit in the package, multiply its speed
(expressed in minutes per field) by the distance and by 60 (to get seconds),
then divide by GAME_SPEED:

    travelSec = distance * slowestSpeedMinPerField * 60 / gameSpeed

Therefore, a package with a Tank (speed 11) and Light infantry (speed 18) moves
at the pace of the Light infantry. Important: on the way back, the time is
recomputed based on the survivors — if only the tanks made it out, the trip home
becomes shorter. Likewise, Hackers have their own speed (9 min/field), so a SPY
command is not constrained by the rest of the army.

For RESOURCES (via HARBOR) we have a global constant of 2 minutes per field
(RESOURCE_TRAVEL_MIN_PER_FIELD): merchants do not depend on the city's units and
must be reasonably fast, otherwise the logistics between your own cities become
prohibitive.

All these formulas live in shared/gameConfig.ts (getFieldDistance,
getSlowestUnitSpeed, getUnitTravelTimeSec, getResourceTravelTimeSec) so there is
no drift between client and server — the client uses them for preview ETAs, the
server for the real BullMQ job delay.


================================================================================
CONQUERING A CITY
================================================================================

A Governor does not conquer on its own — it only reduces loyalty when its package
clears all the defense. Each surviving Governor lowers loyalty by 20-35%
(random roll per Governor). When loyalty reaches 0 or below, the following
happens, atomically, in the same transaction:

  1) One Governor is consumed from the package (the rest remain).
  2) Loyalty resets to 100.
  3) The city's ownerId changes to the attacking player.
  4) All "ARRIVED" SUPPORT commands from other players that were stationed in the
     city are "displaced" back home: each command recomputes its travel time
     based on its own distance and its own slowest unit, then is re-scheduled as
     a return job. This is important: they do not disappear and do not stay in the
     city under the new owner.
  5) The attacker's survivors (minus the consumed Governor) do NOT become units of
     the new city. Instead, a SUPPORT command with status "ARRIVED" is created
     from the attacker's city to the newly conquered city.
     The reportHidden* flags are both true so as not to generate a redundant
     "support arrived" report.

Why is the garrison a SUPPORT and not "troops on the city"?

  - they must contribute to the defense if someone counterattacks immediately
    (the defense logic treats stationed SUPPORT as a defender),
  - but the new owner must NOT be able to use them as if they were their own
    troops: they must not be able to send them to attack, they must not appear in
    the recruitment/total lists,
  - the attacker must be able to withdraw them with the standard withdraw
    mechanism,
  - if another attacker clears the defense (including this garrison), the command
    disappears naturally through the same code path as any other lost SUPPORT.

By reusing the SUPPORT "ARRIVED" infrastructure we get all these behaviors for
free without duplicating logic.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Governor deposit (per-account counter)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Governor counter is per account, not per city. The Nth Governor produced on
the account costs the same regardless of where it was "finalized", and the cost
grows following the formula from the section above (×1.5 up to N=5, then ×2 from
N=6).

The physical recruitment is done in three steps:

  1) The player deposits resources (Money, Energy, Ammo) from any of their own
     cities into the three account-shared progress bars. POST /api/governor/deposit
     subtracts the resources from the source city and raises the corresponding bar.
  2) Deposits can be partial and can come from different cities — useful when a
     single city doesn't have enough of one resource, but combining two does.
  3) When all 3 bars are full, the player presses "Recruit governor".
     POST /api/governor/recruit creates the GOVERNOR unit in the city from which
     the call was made (by default: the city the client was active in), increments
     the producedCount counter, and resets the deposit to zero. The cost of the
     next Governor automatically becomes cost(N+1).

Governors lost in failed attacks stay counted in producedCount — they are not
decremented. This is a design decision: we want the economic pressure to grow
strictly monotonically, so you can't "reset" the price through self-sabotage.


================================================================================
MULTIPLE CITIES PER ACCOUNT
================================================================================

A player can own any number of cities — the starting city plus any ghost or enemy
city conquered with a Governor. At any moment the client has a single "active"
city: all calls of the type GET /api/cities/mine, recruitments, building upgrades
and commands are scoped to the active city.

Persistence and synchronization:

  - activeCityId is saved in localStorage (the key "activeCityId") and at the
    same time mirrored in the URL as ?cityId=... A refresh keeps the same
    selection, and navigating through a direct link knows from the start which
    city to load (the default city no longer flickers).
  - CityPage uses urlCityId with a fallback to localStorage, and on every city
    fetch updates localStorage to the real id received (not to what was
    requested) — so deleted or re-conquered cities don't leave dead references.
  - React Query uses the key ["city", activeCityId ?? "default"] so it doesn't
    mix caches between cities. On switch, we invalidate the ["city"] and
    ["commands"] prefixes to force a refetch without dropping other queries.

Interface:

  - ResourceBar shows a "▾" dropdown next to the city name only if
    ownedCities.length > 1. The dropdown lists all cities with coordinates and a
    marker (●) on the active one.
  - On the map, clicking a city of yours that is NOT the active one opens the
    action panel with two buttons: "Select" (only changes the active city and you
    stay on the map) and "Enter" (takes you to /city?cityId=...). Clicking the
    active city shows no switch button — it's already active.
  - Also on the action panel of a non-active city of yours, the "Support" and
    "Resources" buttons appear, so you can send internal support or move
    resources between your own cities. The active city does not show these buttons
    toward itself (A cannot send to A).
  - Renaming uses PATCH /api/cities/mine/name with { name, cityId }.
    BuildingsView explicitly passes the city.id of the currently viewed city —
    this fixes the bug where renaming a newly conquered city actually affected the
    default city (the first one in ownedCities ordered by createdAt).


================================================================================
COMMANDS: WITHDRAW, CANCEL AND REPORTS
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Withdraw (pulling back stationed SUPPORT)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When you have sent SUPPORT that arrived in another city (status ARRIVED), you can
request the withdrawal: full or partial, per unit. The withdrawal computes a
travel time using the real distance between the target city and the home city,
with the speed of the slowest unit in the withdrawn package. In "all" mode, if
you have multiple SUPPORT commands stationed in the same city, each command gets
its own delay (no batching that would move at the speed of the slowest combined
package).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cancellations (building / recruitment / commands)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Cancelling a build or recruitment command: 75% of the resources are refunded,
    25% is lost. The Level does not change so the score stays the same.
  - Cancelling an ATTACK/SUPPORT/RESOURCES/SPY command that is in TRAVELING:
    available only in the first 5 minutes after departure (a regret window for
    obvious mistakes). After those 5 minutes, the command is "committed" —
    otherwise players could cancel it upon seeing a counterattack and would ruin
    the whole dynamic. After cancellation, the troops return "symmetrically": as
    much time as they traveled to that point, that's how much they have left to
    get home.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Reports (soft-hide per user)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

We don't save duplicate rows for each participant in a battle — we have a single
Command row with the battle result, and two boolean flags
reportHiddenByAttacker and reportHiddenByDefender. A user who presses
"Hide report" sets only the flag corresponding to their role, and the other
participant still sees the report until they decide to hide it too.

Categories:
  - ATTACK (attacker + defender, each sees their own report)
  - SPY (attacker only; the defender does not find out they were spied on)
  - SUPPORT (arrival confirmation for the sender, plus a notification for the
    receiver)
  - RESOURCES (arrival confirmation, delivered amounts)

The client keeps a per-user counter of unread reports (the key is the user id
from the JWT) so the state doesn't mix between accounts that logged in on the
same browser.

On hover over the buildings in the city overview, besides the building name also
